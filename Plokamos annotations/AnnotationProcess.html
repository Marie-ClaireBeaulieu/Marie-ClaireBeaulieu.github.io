Annotation Process
How to annotate

  1. Navigate to Smith's Dictionary using this link
  2. Login to Perseids using the "Login" link at the right topmost part of the screen
  4. Click on "Smith" then "English"
  5. Find the entry you wish to annotate in the alphabetical list. This should be a figure present on the object you are analyzing for your paper or a related figure you wish to learn more about Be careful, many figures have the same name in Greek mythology! Read the entry before starting your work!
  6. Select the word you want to annotate and click the gear icon on the pop-up, then the plus sign
  7. Enter the values in the form using the pre-populated matches. To make sure you have the right figures in each, check the bottom right corner for the stable ID of the person (highlighted in screenshot below). Confirm that the ID of the entry you are annotating corresponds to the entry you are selecting in the drop-down menu.
  8. To find the correct relationship in the middle field, check the list of SNAP relationships
  9. Click "apply"
  10. View your annotation as a popover on hover and as a graph
  11. Hovering over annotations makes the popover show up, clicking it makes it stay (and disappear with another click). Then you can use the gear button to edit the annotation, delete it or create another one.
  12. Similarly, clicking the button for the network diagram makes it stay (and disappear with another click).
  13. When you are finished, commit by clicking the cloud icon on the bottom left
  14. On the appointed date, submit the nickname you entered for your group on the Trunk assignment list. Be sure to include your commentary as a Microsoft Word document
  15. Once you have received instructor feedback, integrate what you have learned from your annotation work to your term project
