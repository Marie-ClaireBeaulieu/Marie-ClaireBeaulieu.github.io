Characterizations
What did the ancients say about this figure?

  1. Select a name for annotation in your Smith entry
  2. Open a new Perseus window and search for the figure you want to annotate with a characterization

Example: A search for Glaucus in Perseus(in a case where you mean to search for the son of Sisyphus) returns entries on Glaucus of Carystus and Glaucus' exchange with Diomedes in the Iliad. Also returns a passage of Strabo, 9.2.24 describing how Glaucus of Potniae (the son of Sisyphus) was torn up by his own mares. Since this passage describes the figure you are looking for, select the Greek word that corresponds to "torn up" (διασπασθέντα) as a characterization

  • In Perseus, find the Greek or Latin word (or phrase) that describes your figure and its English equivalent
  • Copy the Citation URI from the Perseus page on which you found the text. E.g. http://data.perseus.org/citations/urn:cts:greekLit:tlg0099.tlg001.perseus-grc1:9.2.23>
  • Append the actual text you want to reference, separating with an @
  • Example: http://data.perseus.org/citations/urn:cts:greekLit:tlg0099.tlg001.perseus-grc1:9.2.23@διασπασθέντα
  • Find the English equivalent on Perseus and insert its URI with the corresponding English text
  • Example: http://data.perseus.org/citations/urn:cts:greekLit:tlg0099.tlg001.perseus-eng1:9.2.23@torn to pieces
  1. Return to Plokamos
  2. Select the subtitle of your entry "A Dictionary of Greek and Roman biography and mythology. William Smith, LLD, ed. 1890" and double click on it
  3. On the popover, click the button for characterizations
  4. The name of the figure and the identifier for the Smith text fragment will be prepopulated
  5. In the third box, enter the URI for the Greek/Latin term
  6. Do this process again for the English term

This process allows us to display the words that the ancients associated with the figure you are annotating
